Martin Norgaard

I am a biomedical engineer and neuroscientist working to understand and improve reliability and reproducibility in medical image analyses. I have a particular interest in understanding how methodological variability affects the conclusions of neuroimaging data, and how open science and modern software practices can contribute to this. I completed my bachelor's and master's degree in biomedical engineering in (2013 and 2015, respectively) at the Technical University of Denmark (DTU) and my PhD in Neuroscience with Gitte M. Knudsen at the Neurobiology Research Unit in Copenhagen and the University of Copenhagen (2019). During my PhD I spent 5 months in the Laboratory for Computational Neuroimaging at the Martinos Center at MGH/Harvard/MIT working with Doug Greve. After my PhD I was a DFF International Postdoctoral Fellow with Russell A. Poldrack at Stanford University (2022). As of 2023, I am working as an Assistant Professor at the Department of Computer Science, University of Copenhagen, where I teach the course "data analysis and machine learning".
